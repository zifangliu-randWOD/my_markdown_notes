## Program Design Philosophy

### Core Principle: "Position Before Load, Load Before Speed"

Your Olympic lifting numbers are capped by positional efficiency, not raw strength. A 190 kg squat should support a snatch well above 105 kg. This program front-loads technique and positional work when fatigue is low and motor learning is optimal, then layers intensity on top of better movement patterns.

### The 5 Pillars

**1. Olympic Lift Positioning**
Every Oly session begins with complexes that force correct positions — pauses, tempo pulls, hang variations. Volume is high in Phase 1, intensity climbs through Phase 2 and 3. Snatch gets priority over clean because the efficiency gap is larger.

**2. Squat Strength as the Engine**
You squat 3-4x/week across back squat, front squat, and overhead squat. These aren't isolated "squat days" — they serve the Oly lifts. Front squat supports clean recovery. OHS supports snatch stability. Back squat is the raw horsepower builder.

**3. Left-Side Rehab (Background, Non-Negotiable)**
Every session includes unilateral work in the accessory window: Bulgarian split squats, single-leg RDLs, Peterson step-downs, Spanish squats. Runs from Week 1 to Week 24. Goal: by Phase 2, the left knee is no longer a limiting factor.

**4. Thoracic Mobility as a Prerequisite**
Daily thoracic and front rack mobility in the 15-20 min accessory window. This directly unlocks jerk position and clean receiving position. If front rack doesn't improve, C&J will stall regardless of strength.

**5. Conditioning Maintained, Not Prioritized**
Short metcons (8-15 min) appear 2-3x/week, decreasing to 1x in Phase 3. Enough to maintain your engine without interfering with strength and skill adaptations.

### Gymnastics Approach

Ring muscle-ups and handstand walks are skill acquisitions, not strength problems. You have bar muscle-ups and strict HSPU — the strength is there. These get 5-10 min of deliberate practice 2x/week, placed in warm-ups or between heavy sets when neurally primed but not fatigued.

### Weekly Frequency Logic

| Movement | Frequency | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Snatch variations | 2-3x | Primary goal, needs volume for motor learning |
| Clean variations | 2x | Wednesday stays, plus 1 more session |
| Jerk variations | 2x | Built independently before combining with clean |
| Back squat | 1-2x | Raw strength builder |
| Front squat | 1-2x | Clean-specific strength |
| Overhead squat | 1x | Snatch receiving stability |
| Unilateral / prehab | 5x | Every session, accessory window |
| Thoracic mobility | 5x | Every session, warm-up or accessory |
| Gymnastics skill | 2x | Ring MU + HS walk practice |
| Metcon | 2-3x (→1x) | Maintenance, decreasing into Phase 3 |
