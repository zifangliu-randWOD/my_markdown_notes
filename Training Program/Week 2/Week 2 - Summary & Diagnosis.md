## Key Technical Developments

**1. Squat-receive cue is embedding.**
Tuesday and Friday snatches were all caught in a full squat. Friday's C&J was squat-cleaned. This is a fundamental shift from Week 1 where everything was power-caught. The motor pattern is becoming default rather than conscious.

**2. Snatch positions maturing — new refinement territory.**
The issues identified Friday (bar drifting from thigh, bar rolling to palm, forward lean at bottom) are refinement-level problems, not fundamental ones. They only surfaced because the athlete slowed down at the catch — which means the gross motor pattern is solid enough that the fine details are now visible. This is progress.

**3. Jerk grip exploration data gathering.**
Three data points: Thu warm-up push press (wider grip felt good), Thu set 5 push jerk (pinky wider, felt good), Fri C&J sets 3-4 (wider grip from clean catch, limited by hand repositioning). The wider grip direction is promising but the transition from clean catch to jerk grip is a practical blocker. Week 3 will experiment with cleaning in the wider grip directly.

**4. Jerk dip mechanics identified as the primary development area.**
Dip depth inconsistency, forward COM drift, and the resulting knee pain are all connected. The cue "heels heavy through the dip" was introduced Thursday. The Friday C&J had no dip pain — potentially because the squat clean sets up better thoracic engagement than the rack. This is a hypothesis to test in Week 3.

**5. Tempo squat RPE remains elevated.**
115 kg tempo back squat at RPE 8-8.5 (target 6-7) is the most persistent gap in the program. Contributing factors: breathing strategy (holding breath too long), tempo tax (~10-15% effective load), and residual fatigue. Week 3 prescription is already dropped to 110 kg as a safety net. The breathing fix is the primary intervention.
