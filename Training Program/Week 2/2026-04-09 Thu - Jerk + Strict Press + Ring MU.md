> [!info]- Warm-up Movement Guide (click to expand)
>
> **90/90 Breathing — 2 min (NEW — added Week 2)**
> Lie on your back, feet on a wall, knees and hips at 90 degrees. Exhale fully through your mouth, flattening your lower back into the floor. Hold the flat-back position for 3-4 breath cycles (inhale through nose, exhale through mouth, maintain flat back). This teaches the deep core to posteriorly tilt the pelvis against the chronic erector/stabilizer stiffness identified in the Wed PM session. Do this first — it resets pelvic position before loading. Over time this reduces the lumbar-pelvic constraint you feel at the bottom of front squats.
>
> **Banded Lat Stretch x 45 sec each**
> Same as Week 1. Band overhead, grip with one hand, step away for a deep lat stretch. Let your torso rotate slightly toward the band. This directly targets the lat tightness that limits your front rack and overhead position. Spend extra time on the left side — it's more restricted.
>
> **Front Rack Stretch with Barbell x 60 sec**
> Same setup as Week 1. Bar in the rack at front rack height, step in, push elbows up as high as you can. Hold. This is your most important warm-up drill given the thoracic restriction. Compare left to right — is the gap narrowing from Week 1? Even small changes matter.
>
> **Thoracic Rotation on Wall x 8 each side**
> Stand sideways to wall, inside arm against the wall, rotate torso away. Find the sticky point and breathe into it. Better rotation = better ability to get elbows through in front rack.
>
> **Push Press with Empty Bar x 10**
> Dip and drive with empty bar. Standard clean grip. Focus on vertical dip, torso upright, fast lockout.
>
> **NEW — Grip Exploration: Push Press x 5 with Wider Grip**
> After your standard push press warm-up, move your hands out about 1 inch on each side (total ~2 inches wider than clean grip). Do 5 push presses at this width. Pay attention to:
> - Does the dip feel different? (It should feel slightly more natural — the wider grip allows the forearms to be more vertical)
> - Does the lockout overhead feel more or less stable? (Wider = wider base overhead, which should be *more* stable)
> - Does your left shoulder resist the wider position? (If yes, that's the thoracic restriction — note it but don't force it)
>
> This is pure exploration. You're not committing to the wider grip for working sets. Just gathering data on how your body responds. If it feels good, try it on set 4 or 5 of the push jerk. If it feels wrong, stay at clean grip — no harm done.
>
> **Jerk Footwork Drills x 10**
> Without a bar, dip and drive feet into your push jerk receiving position. Focus on fast feet, front shin vertical.

> [!info]- Push Jerk — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Week 2 progression:**
> From 84 kg to 86 kg — a 2.4% jump. This is intentionally small. Week 1 at 84 kg was RPE 6 across all 5 sets, which is ideal. At 86 kg, RPE should land around 6-6.5. The dip-drive mechanics are the same; you're just asking the body to produce slightly more force.
>
> **Compressed week consideration:**
> This session is one day later than usual (Thursday instead of Tuesday), and it follows yesterday's clean and deadlift work. Your quads, glutes, and lower back may carry residual fatigue from yesterday's tempo deadlifts and clean pulls. This can make the jerk dip feel heavier or slower than expected — that's accumulated fatigue, not a technique regression. If sets 1-2 feel like RPE 7+, drop to 82 kg for the remaining sets. Better to drill good dip mechanics at a lighter weight than grind through heavy dips with compromised form.
>
> **Dip — Week 1 feedback integration:**
> On Friday you noted the dip felt "slow and tentative, partly from fear of pain." This is the key development area for your jerk. Here's the approach for Week 2:
> - **Don't force speed.** A controlled, vertical dip at moderate speed is better than a fast, forward-leaning dip.
> - **Focus on depth consistency.** The dip should be 4-6 inches, same depth every rep. If you're varying depth (sometimes shallow, sometimes deep), that's the apprehension showing up as inconsistency.
> - **Cue: "sit the hips, then drive."** Instead of thinking about the knees (which triggers your pain awareness), think about sitting your hips straight down an inch and then exploding up. This shifts your mental focus from the pain site to the movement goal.
> - Your left knee was 4/5 on Friday specifically during the jerk dip. At 86 kg, the dip load is slightly higher — if the knee talks, widen your stance by 1 inch (each foot) to reduce patellar compression.
>
> **If you explored the wider grip during warm-up and it felt good:**
> Try it on sets 4 or 5 only. Keep sets 1-3 at your familiar clean grip so you have a performance baseline to compare against. Note whether the wider grip changes your dip feel, drive efficiency, or lockout stability.
>
> **Expected RPE at 86 kg:** RPE 6-6.5 (may be 7 due to yesterday's session). If it's 7.5+, check the bar path — is it traveling straight up, or is it drifting forward? Forward drift = dip quality issue, not strength issue.

## Secondary: Strict Press

4 x 6 @ 52 kg
Focus: full lockout overhead, no excessive back lean
*Recalibrated from Week 1: 60 kg was RPE 8-9 with failed reps. 50 kg was sustainable. Building +2 kg.*

| Set | Weight | Reps | RPE | Notes |
| --- | ------ | ---- | --- | ----- |
| 1   | 52 kg  |  /6  |     |       |
| 2   | 52 kg  |  /6  |     |       |
| 3   | 52 kg  |  /6  |     |       |
| 4   | 52 kg  |  /6  |     |       |

> [!info]- Strict Press — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Week 2 context:**
> Week 1 taught us your strict press working weight for sets of 6 is ~50 kg, not the initially prescribed 60 kg. This is roughly 45% of your bench (110 kg) — a normal ratio. We add 2 kg this week to 52 kg.
>
> **Why only +2 kg?** Strict press progresses slowly — it's a small muscle group lift with no leg assistance. +2 kg per week on a working weight of 50 kg is a 4% jump, which is appropriate. If you try +5 kg, you'll repeat Week 1's pattern (RPE too high, failed reps, drop weight, frustration).
>
> **Execution is the same as Week 1:**
> - Bar at front rack, elbows slightly forward
> - Squeeze glutes, brace core before every rep ("ribs down, glutes tight")
> - Press straight up — push head through as bar passes forehead
> - Full lockout: elbows straight, active press up, slight shoulder shrug
> - Lower under control
>
> **Watch for:** If your back starts arching excessively on reps 4-6, the shoulders are fatiguing and you're compensating with your lower back. Cue "ribs down" harder on the later reps. If you can't complete set 4 at 52 kg, drop to 50 kg and add a 5th set — same strategy as Week 1.
>
> **Expected RPE at 52 kg:** RPE 6-7 for sets 1-3, maybe 7-7.5 for set 4. You should complete all 4x6 without failure.

## Gymnastics Skill: Ring Muscle-Up Progressions (5-10 min)

- [ ] Deep ring dips: 3 x 6 (slow negative, 3 sec down)
- [ ] False grip hang: 3 x 20 sec
- [ ] Banded ring MU transitions: 3 x 3 (lighter band if possible)

> [!info]- Ring Muscle-Up Progressions — Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Week 2 progression:**
> - Ring dips: 5 reps → 6 reps (same slow negative). One more rep per set builds endurance in the bottom position.
> - False grip hang: 15 sec → 20 sec. Week 1 identified grip as the current limiter. Adding 5 seconds per set increases forearm endurance in the false grip position. If 20 sec is too long — hands slide off the rings — it means the forearm flexors are still adapting. Do what you can hold with good form; even 16-17 sec is progress.
> - Banded transitions: same reps, but try a lighter band if available. If you only have one band, use it — the progression comes from getting more comfortable with the movement pattern.
>
> **What to track:** Is the false grip easier than Week 1? Can you feel the wrists settling more naturally on the rings? This is the early sign that grip endurance is improving.
>
> **Progression tracker from Week 1:**
> - Limiter identified: false grip / forearm endurance
> - Pull height: not yet tested as a limiter
> - Transition: band-assisted, still learning the pattern

## Accessory Window (15-20 min)

- [ ] Banded front rack stretch: 2 x 45 sec each side
- [ ] Prone Y-T-W raises: 3 x 10 each position
- [ ] External rotation with band: 3 x 12 each arm

> [!info]- Accessory — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Banded Front Rack Stretch 2x45 sec each side**
> Same as Week 1. Band at elbow height, pulling your elbow into a deeper front rack position. Spend extra time on the left. This directly supports the jerk grip exploration — a more mobile front rack makes the wider jerk grip accessible over time.
>
> **Prone Y-T-W Raises 3x10 each position**
> Up from 3x8 in Week 1. Same movements (Y for lower traps, T for mid-traps, W for external rotators). The extra 2 reps per position build overhead stability endurance — these are the muscles that keep the bar locked out overhead in the jerk. Use very light weight or bodyweight. If you need to add weight to feel it, you're probably compensating with momentum — slow down instead.
>
> **External Rotation with Band 3x12 each arm**
> Same as Week 1. Elbow pinned to side, rotate forearm outward against band. Prehab for the rotator cuff as pressing volume builds. Control the return — don't let the band snap your arm back.
