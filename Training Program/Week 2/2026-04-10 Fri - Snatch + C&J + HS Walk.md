# Friday — Week 2: Snatch + C&J + HS Walk Skill

[[00 - Program Overview]] | [[Phase 1 - Weeks 1-4]]

> [!tip] Day Focus
> Friday is the **full lifts day** — the culmination of the week. Tuesday you drilled pause snatch positions with complexes; today you put it all together with full snatches at a slightly higher intensity (77%, 81 kg heavy single). This is the last session of the compressed 4-day week (plus the Wed PM supplementary), so accumulated fatigue may show up — especially in the quads after Wednesday's double session. The priority is quality reps with speed, not grinding. The big experiment today: **try the wider jerk grip on C&J sets 3-4** if Thursday's grip exploration felt good. ## Warm-up (10 min)

- [ ] **90/90 breathing: 2 min** (feet on wall, flatten lower back into floor, 3-4 breath cycles per set)
- [ ] Snatch grip RDL x 5
- [ ] Hang snatch high pull x 5
- [ ] Snatch balance x 5
- [ ] Clean & jerk with empty bar x 3

> [!info]- Warm-up Movement Guide (click to expand)
>
> **90/90 Breathing — 2 min**
> Lie on your back, feet on a wall, knees and hips at 90 degrees. Exhale fully through your mouth, flattening your lower back into the floor. Hold the flat-back position for 3-4 breath cycles. Resets pelvic position before loading — addresses the lumbar-pelvic stiffness identified in the Wed PM session.
>
> **Snatch Grip RDL x5**
> Builds awareness of the pulling position with a snatch-width grip. Feel the hamstrings load as you hinge. Keep the bar close to your legs, shoulders over the bar, back flat. This grooves the first pull position — the exact setup you need before every snatch today.
>
> **Hang Snatch High Pull x5**
> From the hip, extend hard and pull the bar high — elbows up and out, bar to chest height. This trains the aggressiveness of the second pull without worrying about catching. Think "fast elbows, high elbows." This primes your nervous system for the explosive pulling you'll need in the full snatches.
>
> **Snatch Balance x5**
> The most important warm-up drill for speed under the bar. Start with bar on back, dip and drive, then punch yourself under into a full overhead squat. This primes the exact motor pattern for the squat-receive cue. After Tuesday's pause snatches, your overhead squat position should feel more dialed in — check if that improvement carried over.
>
> **Clean & Jerk with Empty Bar x3**
> Rehearse the full clean and jerk movement. Focus on the transition — clean, recover, reset feet, dip and jerk. If you experimented with the wider jerk grip on Thursday, try it here at empty bar too. Pay attention to your front rack position as you catch the clean — elbows up, thoracic extended.

> [!info]- Snatch (Full Lifts) — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Why full snatches today?**
> Tuesday's complex broke the snatch into pieces — pause at the knee, full lift, OHS. Today, you put it together as one fluid lift. The goal is to apply what you practiced: patient off the floor, controlled transition through the knee, higher contact point, fast turnover, and squat-receive. This is where the complex work pays off.
>
> **Building to a heavy single at 77% (81 kg)**
> Build up gradually: empty bar → 50 kg → 60 kg → 70 kg → 76 kg → 81 kg. Each weight should feel easy before you add. The heavy single is NOT a max attempt — it's a "comfortable heavy" that reinforces positions under slightly more load. This is 2 kg heavier than Week 1's heavy single (79 kg). If 81 kg doesn't feel right, stop at 79 kg and move on.
>
> **Working sets 5x2 at 72-75% (76-79 kg)**
> Pick a weight in this range based on how the heavy single felt. If 81 kg was smooth, use 79 kg for working sets. If 81 kg was a grind, drop to 76 kg. Two reps per set means you get a second chance to correct anything from rep 1. Full reset between reps.
>
> **Week 2 squat-receive cue — this is critical:**
> Week 1 you power snatched everything including the 79 kg single. That confirmed your pull and extension are strong at these loads. But at 80%+, the inability to pull yourself under becomes the limiter. This week, on every working set rep, ride the bar down into a full overhead squat catch even if you have the power to catch it high. This is deliberate practice for the receiving position. If you find yourself catching in a power position, immediately ride it down to full depth before standing — build the motor pattern.
>
> **Connection to the snatch efficiency gap:**
> At 55% snatch-to-squat ratio, the lever is technique, not strength. The pause snatch work from Tuesday should have reinforced the transition through the knee. Today, look for: (1) patient off the floor, (2) bar stays close, (3) fast turnover into a deep overhead catch. Did the higher contact point from Tuesday carry over?
>
> **Expected RPE:**
> Heavy single at 81 kg: RPE 6-7. Working sets at 76-79 kg: RPE 5-7. These should feel smooth and fast. If any rep feels like RPE 8+, you're either pulling wrong or fatigued from the compressed week. Drop weight rather than grind.
>
> **Compressed week consideration:**
> This is day 4 of 4 with no rest day in between. Accumulated fatigue from Tuesday's squats, Wednesday's deadlifts, and Thursday's pressing may show up as slightly slower reflexes or heavier-feeling snatches. If that happens, it's fatigue, not regression. Keep the working weights honest — RPE is the guide, not the programmed percentage.

> [!info]- Clean & Jerk — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Week 2 progression:**
> Working weight goes from 93 kg to 95 kg — a modest 2 kg bump. The clean at 95 kg should still feel comfortable (68% of 140 kg clean). The jerk at 95 kg is closer to the upper limit (79% of ~120 kg jerk), which means the jerk remains the more challenging component. This gap between clean comfort and jerk difficulty is expected and reflects the priority order: jerk mechanics are being built gradually.
>
> **The jerk grip experiment (sets 3-4):**
> Thursday's warm-up included grip exploration with the push press. If the wider grip felt good there:
> - Sets 1-2: use your familiar clean grip. This establishes a baseline for how 95 kg feels today.
> - Sets 3-4: widen your hands ~1 inch each side. Pay attention to three things:
>   1. Does the dip feel different? (Wider grip = more vertical forearms = potentially cleaner dip)
>   2. Does the drive transfer better? (Vertical forearms transmit force more efficiently)
>   3. Does the overhead lockout feel more or less stable?
> - If the wider grip feels worse on set 3, go back to clean grip for set 4. No need to force it — this data informs the Week 3 approach.
>
> If Thursday's grip exploration felt wrong or unstable, skip the experiment entirely and use clean grip for all 4 sets. There's no rush.
>
> **Clean execution:**
> Smooth pull to hip, fast elbows in the catch. At 95 kg, focus on the turnover — elbow speed in the catch is the quality differentiator. Stand up fully, reset feet, breathe, then jerk.
>
> **Jerk execution:**
> Vertical dip, consistent depth (4-6 inches), fast lockout. Thursday's session revealed **COM forward drift during the dip** causing mild bilateral knee pain (left ~2-3/10). Primary cue for today: **"heels heavy through the dip"** — keep pressure across the whole foot, not just the balls. If you feel pressure shift to toes, the dip is going forward.
> Thursday's wider grip experiment (pinky-finger wider) felt good on set 5 — try it again on sets 2-3 today to gather more data.
>
> **Expected RPE:**
> Clean: RPE 5-6. Jerk: RPE 6-7. If the jerk feels harder than the clean, that's expected. If the jerk feels like RPE 8+, the compressed week is catching up — drop to 90 kg for the remaining sets.

## Gymnastics Skill: Handstand Walk Progressions (5-10 min)

- [ ] Wall-facing handstand hold: 3 x 35 sec
- [ ] Wall walk with shoulder taps: 3 x 6 each hand
- [ ] Freestanding handstand kick-up attempts: 5 attempts (try to hold 3 sec)

> [!info]- Handstand Walk Progressions — Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Week 2 progression:**
> - Wall holds: 30 sec → 35 sec. Five more seconds per set builds shoulder endurance in the inverted position. If 35 sec feels easy, move your hands closer to the wall for a more vertical body position.
> - Shoulder taps: 5 → 6 each hand. One more rep per side continues building the lateral weight shift needed for walking. Go slow — control over speed.
> - Freestanding kick-ups: same 5 attempts, but now try to hold each kick-up for 3 seconds. Week 1 was about getting upside down; Week 2 is about staying there briefly.
>
> **Week 1 review:**
> Wall holds and shoulder taps were completed and felt solid. Freestanding kick-ups were "still inconsistent." That's normal for Week 2 — most athletes don't have reliable freestanding holds until 4-6 weeks of consistent practice. The attempt IS the training stimulus.
>
> **Compressed week note:**
> This comes at the end of a 4-day block with no rest days. Your shoulders have been worked Thursday (press + jerk). If shoulder fatigue limits your hold times, that's fine — log actual hold times vs target so we can track progress week over week. Handstand work is knee-friendly and a welcome mental break after the Oly lifts.

## Accessory Window (15-20 min)

- [ ] Peterson step-downs: 3 x 10 each leg (5 kg DB)
- [ ] Pigeon stretch: 2 x 45 sec each side
- [ ] 90/90 hip rotation: 2 x 8 each side

> [!info]- Accessory — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Peterson Step-Downs 3x10 each leg (5 kg DB)**
> This appears twice per week (Tuesday and Friday) because patellar tendon rehab needs consistent, frequent loading. Same progression as Tuesday: hold a 5 kg dumbbell at your chest, left leg first, 3-second controlled descent, knee tracking straight over toes. Compare how the left knee feels at end-of-week vs start-of-week — is it stable, worse, or improving?
>
> **Pigeon Stretch 2x45 sec each side**
> Targets the deep external rotators of the hip (piriformis, etc.). Tight hips limit squat depth and can cause the knees to cave inward. Get into a pigeon position (front shin across your body, back leg extended behind), keep hips square, and fold forward until you feel a deep stretch. Breathe and relax into it. Don't force depth — gravity and time do the work.
>
> **90/90 Hip Rotation 2x8 each side**
> Sit on the floor with both legs at 90-degree angles. Rotate your legs to switch which hip is externally rotated and which is internally rotated. This builds active hip mobility — the ability to move through full hip range under control. Note if one direction feels significantly tighter — that's likely the side that limits your squat symmetry. Move slowly and own each position.
