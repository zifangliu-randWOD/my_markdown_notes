## Warm-up (12 min)

- [ ] Snatch grip RDL: 5 reps
- [ ] Hang snatch high pull: 5 reps
- [ ] Snatch balance: 5 reps
- [ ] Clean & jerk with empty bar: 3 reps

> [!info]- Warm-up Movement Guide (click to expand)
>
> **90/90 breathing — 2 min**
> Same daily reset. On this day it has two jobs: keep the right erector quiet after yesterday's front squat re-entry and establish the rib-down position for overhead receiving. If yesterday's session left any lumbar stiffness, add two extra breath cycles before moving to the foam roll.
>
> **Right lumbar erector foam roll — 60 sec**
> Gentle right-side pass. You are not trying to erase soreness before lifting; you are checking whether yesterday created new tonus. If the right side feels clearly worse than May 6 pre-session, note it before the snatch work and keep the single conservative.
>
> **Couch stretch — 60 sec each side**
> Keep ribs down and glute squeezed. This protects the receiving positions by reducing anterior pelvic tilt, and it keeps the jerk dip from turning into a knee-forward, lumbar-extended pattern.
>
> **Snatch grip RDL — 5 reps**
> Hamstring + bar-close-to-shins pattern check. Push hips back, bar stays against the legs, lats tight. This is where the patient first pull starts; if the bar drifts forward here, it will drift forward at 79 kg.
>
> **Hang snatch high pull — 5 reps**
> "Elbows lead, hands follow." Finish the hip drive, then guide the bar vertically to sternum height. Do not lean back to make the bar feel high. This rehearses the second pull without forcing the catch.
>
> **Snatch balance — 5 reps**
> Turnover under the bar. Apply "knuckles to ceiling" on every catch. Stick the bottom position for one second, eyes on the wall. This is the most specific drill for the bar-forward retest because it teaches the body to receive vertically instead of chasing.
>
> **C&J empty bar — 3 reps**
> Squat clean every rep, wider jerk stance default, "heels heavy through the dip." Rehearse the full sequence even with the empty bar: clean, controlled stand-up breath, one-second pause, re-brace, jerk.

> [!info]- Snatch — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **The 84 kg single — what it means:**
> Wk 3 you hit 84 kg @ RPE 7. Today, 4 weeks later, with all the Phase 1 work behind you, 84 kg should feel cleaner and easier. Target RPE: 6.5 — about a half-point easier than Wk 3. If it feels like RPE 7 again, that's still fine; if it feels like RPE 6, you're ahead of schedule.
>
> **Build-up plan:** empty bar x3, 40 kg x3, 55 kg x2, 70 kg x1, 78 kg x1, 84 kg single. After the single, rest 2:30-3:00 before 79 kg doubles. If the 78 kg rep is forward, repeat 78 kg rather than jumping to 84 kg with the same error.
>
> **Working sets at 79 kg — the bar-forward check:**
> Wk 3 Friday's 80 kg working sets failed forward 3/10 reps under fatigue. Today is 79 kg fresh. The expected RPE is 5–6 across all 5 sets. If any rep falls forward, the diagnostic is not a strength issue — it's the second-pull-too-early pattern under accumulated fatigue. **Stay patient in the pull. Finish the hip drive before pulling under.** Your hips are the engine; the arms get out of the way.
>
> **No compensation sets:**
> If a rep is missed, log it and move on. The Wk 3 Friday lesson holds: 9/10 = a pass. No 6th set added.
>
> **Squat-receive every rep:**
> Even the 79 kg singles can power-snatch easily. Don't. The rest of Phase 1 needs the squat-receive habit groomed at every load before Phase 2 gets heavier.
>
> **Anterior shoulder check:**
> Should have closed permanently after Wk 5 Wed Apr 29 (105 kg squat clean). If still absent today, the limiter is fully off the active list.
>
> **What a good 79 kg double feels like:**
> Rep 1 should feel like a technical single, not effortful. Rep 2 should not change pull speed or catch height. If rep 2 catches higher, powers out, or drifts forward, fatigue is entering the pattern. Keep the next rest at a full 2:30 and cue "finish hip drive before pull-under."
>
> **Miss protocol:** if a rep misses forward, write "forward" in the table, take the normal rest, and continue. Do not add a set and do not take an immediate retry. The training outcome is cleaner information, not a perfect made-rep count at any cost.

> [!info]- C&J — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Where 98 kg sits:** clean is 70%, jerk is 82%. The jerk is the harder portion at this weight. RPE target: clean 6.5, jerk 7.
>
> **Squat clean every rep:**
> Wk 4 Wed at 100 kg fresh + Wk 5 Wed at 105 kg both clean = the squat clean receiving pattern is rebuilt. 98 kg today is well within the rebuilt territory. Don't power-clean even one rep.
>
> **Heavy clean stand-up breathing — applied:**
> - At bottom of squat catch: hold brace
> - During stand: controlled exhale through pursed lips, ~50% of breath out by the time standing
> - At top: finish exhale, take 1 quick refill breath, **own the 1-sec pause**
> - Set up jerk: re-brace, dip, drive
>
> **Jerk dive-and-drive:**
> Wk 4 Fri you noted "dive and drive for jerk is sharp, and i like it a lot" — keep that. Wider stance, heels heavy through the dip, vertical drive, lock out fast.
>
> **Bar drop on the descent:**
> At 98 kg, **drop the bar from the front rack to the platform** between reps. Don't try to ride it down. Bar-lowering is the hypothesized aggravator of the Wk 3 anterior shoulder finding; the protocol from Wk 4 review is permanent.
>
> **Knee monitoring:** Wk 5 Tue jerk @ 92 kg should have been knee 5/5 with the wider stance. Today at 98 kg + from squat clean (which has historically been better for the knee than jerk-from-rack) should also be 5/5.
>
> **C&J build-up:** 60 kg x1+1, 80 kg x1+1, 90 kg x1+1, then 98 kg working sets. Every build-up rep uses the bar-drop protocol once the clean is 80 kg or above. This is where the habit gets installed.
>
> **Clean receive checklist:** full squat, elbows through early, eyes on wall, stand with controlled exhale. Do not rush the pause before the jerk; the one-second pause is there to prevent the chain-clean-and-jerk pattern that contributed to lightheadedness.
>
> **Jerk checklist:** wider stance already set, bar in palm, elbows forward enough to keep the bar on the shelf, heels heavy in dip, vertical drive. If the jerk misses or feels forward, check stance and bar-in-hand position first before blaming strength.
>
> **Expected feel:** clean around RPE 6-6.5, jerk around RPE 7. If the clean is harder than the jerk, something is off in the pull or stand-up breathing. If the knee is anything below 5/5, reduce jerk load to 92 kg for remaining sets and keep the clean if it feels clean.

## Gymnastics Skill: Handstand Walk Progressions (5–10 min)

**3 sets each (back to Wk 3 volume after deload + trip):**

- [ ] Wall-facing HS hold: 3 × 40 sec
- [ ] Wall walk with shoulder taps: 3 × 8 each hand
- [ ] Wall-facing HS lateral weight shifts: 3 × 5 each direction
- [ ] Freestanding HS attempts: 5 attempts (aim for 5 sec holds — Wk 2 baseline)

> [!info]- HS Walk — Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Volume: back to 3 sets** after Wk 4's 2-set deload. Skill volume returns to normal for the first full post-trip Oly day.
>
> **Freestanding attempts return:**
> Wk 4 paused freestanding (deload protocol). Today resumes — 5 attempts, aim for the Wk 2 baseline of 5-sec holds. CNS is fresh after the trip, so this is a reasonable test day.
>
> **Post-Oly shoulder check:**
> The snatch + C&J today loaded the shoulders meaningfully. If they feel inflamed or sore heading into HS work, skip the lateral shifts (2 sets of holds only) and note it.
>
> **Wall-facing HS hold:**
> Nose close to wall, ribs down, glutes tight, active shoulders. This should feel like stacked overhead support, not lumbar arch survival. If the low back arches, step the hands 5-10 cm farther from the wall and rebuild the line.
>
> **Wall walk with shoulder taps:**
> Shift weight slowly before tapping. The goal is shoulder loading tolerance and midline control, not speed. Keep the support arm locked and push the floor away.
>
> **Lateral weight shifts:**
> Small shifts only. Think shoulder over wrist, then return to center. If wrists or shoulders feel cooked after C&J, drop this to 2 sets.
>
> **Freestanding attempts:**
> Five attempts means five calm attempts, not five rushed kick-ups. Aim to match Wk 2's 5-sec baseline. Stop if repeated over-kicks create shoulder irritation.
