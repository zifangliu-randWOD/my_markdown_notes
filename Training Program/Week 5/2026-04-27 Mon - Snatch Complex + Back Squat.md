> [!info]- Warm-up Movement Guide (click to expand)
>
> **The new pre-warm-up additions (foam roll + couch stretch)** are not optional this week. Both target the right erector tonus pattern characterized Friday: foam roll reduces local tone in the muscle, couch stretch lengthens the hip flexors that anteriorly tilt the pelvis (which keeps the erectors loaded all day). Two minutes total. Do them every training day.
>
> **90/90 breathing — 2 min**
> Feet on wall, knees and hips at 90 degrees. Exhale until the low back is flat against the floor, then take slow nasal breaths into the posterior ribs and lower back. This is the anchor habit that resolved the bilateral lumbar-pelvic restriction in Wk 3; today it also creates the brace shape you need before belted squats. If the right erector feels busy even during breathing, stay here for one extra breath cycle before moving on.
>
> **Right lumbar erector foam roll — 60 sec**
> Work 2-3 cm to the right of the spine between the bottom rib and the top of the pelvis. Gentle pressure only — this is down-regulation, not deep tissue punishment. The right erector should feel less guarded after 60 seconds. If it feels sharper or more protective, stop the pressure and log it; that would be a different signal than normal tonus.
>
> **Couch stretch — 60 sec each side**
> Back-leg glute squeezed, ribs down, pelvis tucked under. The stretch should live in the front of the hip and upper quad, not in the low back. This matters for today's squat because tight hip flexors pull the pelvis forward, which asks the erectors to stabilize the trunk before the set even starts.
>
> **Thoracic foam roll — 90 sec**
> Work from mid-back to upper thoracic, one segment at a time. Exhale over the roller and let the chest open without arching the lumbar spine. Your thoracic restriction is bilateral but worse on the left; spend an extra breath on any segment where the left side feels stuck. This sets up both the snatch catch and the back-squat shelf.
>
> **Banded pull-aparts — 15 reps**
> Pull to the lower chest with straight elbows, shoulder blades moving back and slightly down. No shrugging. This wakes up the upper back so the snatch bar stays close and the squat bar has a stable shelf.
>
> **PVC snatch-grip BTN press — 8 reps**
> Grip wide enough that the PVC clears behind the head without rib flare. Press up and slightly back until the bar is stacked over midfoot. If the left shoulder feels more restricted, slow the final third of each rep and breathe there.
>
> **OHS empty bar — 5 reps**
> Full-depth position check before loading. Eyes on the wall, knuckles to ceiling, ribs down. If the bar drifts forward at empty bar, do not assume 81 kg will fix it — repeat the warm-up rep until the receive position stacks cleanly.
>
> **Snatch balance — 5 reps**
> Dip straight, drive straight, punch under fast. This is the most specific drill for today's complex because it rehearses receiving the bar after a vertical drive rather than chasing it forward. Stick each catch for one second before standing.

> [!info]- Snatch Complex — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Why this complex:** Three separate "pulls" with different setups force you to feel the bar path at three positions: floor (slow start, patient), knee (3-sec pause = positional discipline), hang above knee (already in the power position — pure hip drive). This is the most position-rich snatch complex in Phase 1. At 77% (81 kg), the load is moderate — the work is in the positions, not the strength.
>
> **How to build to 81 kg:** empty bar complex x2, 40 kg x1, 55 kg x1, 70 kg x1, 76 kg x1, then 81 kg working sets. Do not jump straight from 70 to 81 if the catch feels rusty. The build-up reps are the first diagnostic of the day: if the pause-at-knee position already feels hard at 70, the issue is position, not load.
>
> **Pull to Knee with 3-sec pause:**
> - Set up over the bar — shoulders just slightly ahead of the bar, lats engaged, eyes forward (not down).
> - Pull off the floor with patience — the bar moves at the speed your back stays flat, no faster.
> - Pause 3 seconds at knee height. The bar should be against the shins, your back angle should be the same as the setup, and your shoulders should still be slightly ahead of the bar.
> - The pause is a positional check: if you feel like you have to "fight" to stay over the bar, your first pull is yanking the chest up too early. Reset on the next set.
>
> **Hang Snatch (above knee):**
> - Lower the bar to mid-thigh (above the knee). Hips back, slight knee bend.
> - Drive hard with the hips — the "sweep into hip" cue applies here: the bar should brush the hip crease as the hips fire.
> - Knuckles to ceiling on the receive. Hook grip throughout.
>
> **Snatch from Floor:**
> - Standard full snatch.
> - Apply everything from the previous two reps: patient first pull, vertical second pull, knuckles to ceiling on catch.
>
> **What the 84 kg single in Wk 3 means for today:** It tells you the strength is there and the position holds at 84 kg fresh. 81 kg complex today should feel like RPE 5–6 across the working sets. If it feels harder than that, check the bar path on the snatch from floor — you may be pulling the chest up too early.
>
> **Common failure sequence to catch early:**
> First pull gets rushed → chest rises early → bar separates from the shin → hang snatch starts too far forward → catch has to chase. The 3-sec pause exists to interrupt that sequence. If any set feels forward, the next set's cue is not "pull harder"; it is "hold the back angle to the knee."
>
> **What a good set should feel like:**
> The pause should feel stable, not shaky. The hang snatch should feel like a short, violent vertical extension with the bar brushing the hip. The final snatch should feel easier than the hang rep because the rhythm from the floor is restored. RPE should stay flat; a set-to-set climb means fatigue or bar path leakage.
>
> **Anterior shoulder check:** No anterior shoulder discomfort during the catch. If you feel anything, note it — but the deload + 3 clean fresh data points strongly suggests it's gone. Wednesday's 105 kg squat clean is the natural retest.

**Athlete Notes:** All 5 complexes completed at 82 kg (1 kg above target). Pause position stable, hang snatch above knee with high power position, fast transition. Did squat snatch throughout (not power snatch). On the final snatch from floor, felt explosive — could feel leaving the ground slightly on the third pull. Overall strong and not overly demanding.

> [!info]- Back Squat — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Where 143 kg sits:** Your current PR is 190 kg. 143 kg is 75%. Wk 3's 110 kg @ 5×5 with the breathing fix held RPE 7–7.5; 143 kg today is +33 kg but with belt and full deload behind you. Target RPE: 7–7.5. The breathing protocol you locked in during Wk 3 (between-rep breathing pause, 90/90 carryover) is what makes this load feel like the prescribed RPE rather than an over-target grind.
>
> **Belt on — non-negotiable today.** This is the first day back to belted squats since Wk 3. Going belt-less Wk 4 Mon contributed to the right erector tonus picture; today the belt does the bracing work the erectors were doing alone last week. Set the belt tight enough that you can take a breath into it without it moving.
>
> **Tempo 3-1-0:** 3 seconds down, 1-second pause at the bottom, explode up. The pause at the bottom kills the bounce and forces the quads + hips to start the concentric from a dead stop. That's where the breathing matters most: take the breath at the top, hold it through the descent + pause + ascent, exhale at the top. Reset.
>
> **Right erector self-check:** notice how the right lumbar feels at the bottom of the squat with the belt on. If it's noticeably better than Wk 4 (when you were belt-less), the belt is doing exactly what it should. If it's the same or worse with the belt on, that's important information — note it.
>
> **Eye gaze:** floor 2–3 m ahead (your back squat is naturally a bit forward-leaning, gaze follows torso angle). Not at the floor directly under you, not at the ceiling.
>
> **If RPE climbs above 7.5 by set 2:** stop, breathe, and reduce to 138 kg for the remaining sets. Don't grind RPE 8+ on the first day of Block 2 — the goal is to hit the load with quality, not to overreach.
>
> **Rep-by-rep checklist:**
> 1. Top: inhale into the belt, brace 360 degrees, elbows pinned under the bar.
> 2. Descent: 3 seconds, knees track over toes, full-foot pressure. Do not let the left knee dive inward.
> 3. Bottom: one real second. Stay tight; do not breathe in the hole.
> 4. Ascent: drive the floor away, exhale only after the sticking point or once standing.
> 5. Reset: stand tall, take 1-2 recovery breaths, then re-brace for the next rep.
>
> **Expected feel:** Set 1 should feel controlled and slightly conservative. Set 2 is the truth-set: if the belt and breathing are working, it should still sit around RPE 7. Set 4 can climb, but it should not become a grind. Right lumbar should feel supported, not like it is doing the whole job.

**Athlete Notes:** 143 kg felt like a grind on the first attempt, so dropped to 133 kg for all 4 working sets. Even at 133 kg, the 3-sec eccentric felt like a real struggle — couldn't feel strong enough to do it cleanly. Sets 1–2 were low bar; switched to high bar on set 3 and it felt noticeably better. Not sure which position to commit to going forward. Set 4: too exhausted to hold the 1-sec bottom pause, so removed it and did straight 5 reps at 3-sec eccentric only — RPE dropped a little.

Pick high bar, practice it consistently, and watch the tempo work start to feel more manageable.
