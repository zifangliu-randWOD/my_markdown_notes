# Trip Mobility & Rehab Protocol — Apr 30 → May 5, 2026

[[00 - Program Overview]] | [[Phase 1 - Weeks 5-8]]

> [!tip] Trip Focus
> **6 days off lifting. The trip is your built-in mid-Phase deload between two heavy 3-day blocks.** Equipment: bodyweight + light resistance band(s). Activity tier: Tier 2 — mobility & rehab, no conditioning. The objective is not to "maintain fitness" with random bodyweight work; it is to arrive on May 6 with the two active maintenance items in better shape than they were on Apr 29: **right erector spinae tonus** (newly characterized Wk 4 Fri — chronic muscular pattern) and **left hip ER stiffness** (closing trend). The thoracic + shoulder bilateral restriction, worse on the left, also gets daily attention because it is the shared limiter for front rack, jerk dip, OHS, and snatch receive. Treat the protocol like a daily 20-minute training session: same order, same intent, honest tracking. Success looks boring: no new soreness, right erector calmer, left hip smoother, left overhead position less restricted, and no urge to "make up" training with fatigue work.

---

## Daily Protocol — Same Sequence, Every Day

> [!info] Time required: ~20–25 min total. Order matters — reset first, hips second, thoracic/shoulder third, decompression last. Keep every movement at RPE 2–4. This is tissue quality and position maintenance, not a workout.

### Phase 1 — Reset (3 min)

- [ ] **90/90 breathing — 2 min**
  - Lying on back, feet on wall, knees and hips both at 90°
  - Breathe into the posterior ribcage, allow lumbar to flatten into the floor
  - 8–10 slow breath cycles
  - *Why:* the foundational pelvic reset that resolved the lumbar-pelvic constraint in Wk 3. Also the diaphragmatic activation that supports right erector relaxation.
  - *How it should feel:* quiet and expansive. You should feel the low back soften into the floor and the ribs move down rather than flaring up. If the right erector feels like it is guarding even while lying down, stay for 4–5 extra breaths.
  - *Common mistake:* forcing the low back flat by squeezing the abs hard. The goal is breath-led relaxation, not a plank on your back.

- [ ] **Right lumbar erector self-massage — 60 sec**
  - No foam roller? Use a tennis ball, a water bottle, or just your thumb/knuckle pressed against the wall
  - Find the right erector (rope-like muscle 2–3 cm right of the spine, between bottom rib and iliac crest)
  - Apply firm but not aggressive pressure. Roll/work for 60 seconds.
  - *Why:* manages the chronic right erector tonus pattern characterized Wk 4 Fri.
  - *Pressure target:* 3–4/10 discomfort, never sharp. You are asking the muscle to downshift, not trying to crush it into submission.
  - *What to log:* does it feel calmer afterward, unchanged, or more irritated? "More irritated" means tomorrow's dose should be lighter or skipped.

### Phase 2 — Hip Mobility (5 min)

- [ ] **Couch stretch — 60 sec each side**
  - Standing or kneeling — back foot elevated on a chair/bed/wall, front foot forward in a lunge
  - Squeeze the back-leg glute, posteriorly tilt the pelvis, push hips forward
  - You should feel a deep stretch in the front of the back-leg hip flexor and quad
  - *Why:* anteriorly tight hip flexors anteriorly tilt the pelvis → erectors stay loaded all day. Couch stretch directly releases the pelvis. **Highest-leverage movement in the protocol.**
  - *Setup check:* ribs down, glute squeezed, belt-line tucked slightly upward. If you feel the stretch mostly in the low back, you are arching instead of opening the hip.
  - *Athlete-specific note:* this is the strongest non-barbell support for the right erector plan. Travel sitting loads the hip flexors; this is the daily counter-dose.
  - *Expected feel:* strong quad/hip-flexor stretch, no knee pain. If the knee on the back leg feels compressed, put padding under it or switch to a standing version.

- [ ] **Pigeon stretch — 60 sec each side, LEFT FIRST**
  - Front shin roughly parallel to the front of the mat, back leg extended behind, hips square
  - Sink the front hip toward the floor; for deeper stretch, fold torso forward over the front leg
  - *Why:* left hip ER (external rotation) was the closing finding in Wk 4. Keep it closed during the trip.
  - *Left-side standard:* the left side does not have to match the right in depth; it has to feel smoother by the final 20 seconds. Start with whatever shin angle lets the hip relax without knee torque.
  - *Common mistake:* chasing a parallel shin and twisting the knee. If the knee feels anything other than neutral pressure, pull the heel closer to the body and reduce the angle.
  - *What to log:* use the 1–5 left hip ER score after this drill, not before. We care whether the protocol changes the hip, not only how it wakes up.

- [ ] **90/90 hip rotation — 10 reps each side**
  - Sit on floor, both legs at 90° (front leg externally rotated, back leg internally rotated)
  - Rotate the pelvis to switch sides (the back knee tips forward)
  - Slow and controlled, 10 reps each side
  - *Why:* maintains hip rotational mobility (ER + IR) needed for snatch/clean receive positions.
  - *Execution cue:* tall chest, hands light on the floor only if needed. Rotate the hips, not the spine. The left ER phase is the part to watch.
  - *Expected feel:* controlled, slightly sticky on the left, improving across reps. Clicking without pain is acceptable; pinching or knee torque is not.

### Phase 3 — Thoracic + Shoulder (4 min)

- [ ] **Banded overhead distraction — 30 sec each arm, +10 sec on left**
  - Anchor band overhead (door, tree branch, sturdy fixture)
  - Grip band with one hand, thumb up, step away until band is taut and pulling arm forward and up
  - Allow arm into end-range overhead flexion; keep ribcage down (don't arch lower back)
  - Right: 30 sec. **Left: 40 sec.** (Left is the more restricted side.)
  - *Why:* shoulder joint capsule + thoracic mobility — directly targets the bilateral-worse-on-left thoracic restriction.
  - *Setup check:* thumb points up, elbow soft, ribs down, glute lightly squeezed. If the low back arches, the shoulder is borrowing range from the lumbar spine; step closer to the anchor and reset.
  - *Left-side focus:* the left arm may feel more blocked and less stretchy. That is the known restriction. Stay patient and breathe; do not crank the band angle into an anterior shoulder pinch.
  - *Carryover target:* May 6 OHS and front rack should feel less "left arm stuck" if this is done daily.

- [ ] **Banded shoulder dislocates — 10 reps slow**
  - Stand holding band wide grip (or PVC if you have one); slowly pass band overhead and behind, then return
  - Shoulders stay packed, don't shrug
  - 10 reps with a 3-second pace each direction
  - *Why:* thoracic extension under controlled shoulder flexion — front rack mobility carryover.
  - *Grip width:* wide enough to move without pain, narrow enough to feel useful. Do not force a narrow grip while traveling; this is maintenance, not a mobility test.
  - *Quality cue:* ribs down the whole time. If the band only clears by arching the low back, widen the hands.
  - *Stop signal:* anterior shoulder pinch, numbness, or sharp biceps-tendon sensation. Switch to face pulls only that day.

- [ ] **Banded face pulls — 15 reps**
  - Anchor band at face height. Pull the band toward your face with elbows high, end position is hands by the ears with elbows spread wide.
  - Mid-trap + posterior shoulder activation
  - *Why:* prevents posterior chain detraining during the lifting break. Carries directly to jerk lockout stability when you return.
  - *Execution cue:* pull elbows wide, finish with shoulder blades back and down, pause one second. No lower-back lean to create tension.
  - *Expected feel:* rear delts, mid-traps, lower traps. If you feel neck/upper traps only, lower the anchor or reduce band tension.

### Phase 4 — Spinal Decompression (3 min)

- [ ] **Cat-cow — 10 reps, slow**
  - On hands and knees, alternate spinal flexion (cow → arch back, look up) and extension (cat → round back, tuck chin)
  - Move one vertebra at a time, breathing with each transition
  - *Why:* segmental spinal mobility — opens the lumbar joints between heavy training blocks.
  - *Pace:* 3 seconds into each position, one full breath, then transition. The goal is segmental motion, not reps.
  - *Right erector check:* the right side should feel like it moves more freely by rep 10. If it tightens as you move, shorten the range and slow down.

- [ ] **Standing forward fold — 60 sec**
  - Feet hip-width, slowly hinge forward, let the head and arms hang
  - Slight bend in the knees if the hamstrings are very tight
  - Just hang. Breathe. Let the spine decompress.
  - *Execution cue:* soft knees, heavy head, slow breathing. Do not force the palms to the floor. This is decompression after travel sitting and after the mobility work.
  - *Expected feel:* hamstrings and low-back stretch, mild and broad. Sharp localized lumbar sensation means come out of it and finish with child's pose instead.

### Optional Add-Ons (if you have band tension and a few extra minutes)

- [ ] **Banded supine hip distraction — 60 sec each side**
  - Lie on back, band looped around one foot, band anchored to a low fixed point
  - Let the band pull the hip forward (toward distraction), allowing capsule space
  - Especially helpful if left hip ER feels rough on a given day
  - Use this when the left pigeon stretch does not change the hip ER score by at least one point.

- [ ] **Banded ankle dorsiflexion — 60 sec each side**
  - Band around the front of the ankle, anchored low and forward
  - In a lunge, drive the knee over the toes; band pulls the tibia forward
  - Carries forward to squat depth on return
  - Use this if travel walking/sitting makes the ankles feel blocked before May 6 front squat and OHS.

> [!info]- Daily Execution Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Best time of day:** any consistent time works, but post-walk or pre-bed is ideal. Post-walk means tissues are warm; pre-bed supports down-regulation and may make the right erector quieter overnight.
>
> **Minimum viable version if travel gets chaotic:** 90/90 breathing 2 min, right erector self-massage 60 sec, couch stretch 60 sec each side, pigeon 60 sec left, banded overhead distraction 40 sec left, cat-cow 10 reps. That is the non-negotiable 10-minute version.
>
> **What not to chase:** sweat, burn, soreness, fatigue, or a sense of having "trained." The correct feeling after the protocol is open and boring. If you finish breathing hard, you turned it into conditioning.

## What's Deliberately NOT in This Protocol

- **No conditioning** — no bodyweight squats for sets, no push-ups for sets, no planks for time. You explicitly chose Tier 2.
- **No bodyweight strength work** — no goblet squats, no push-up variations as exercises.
- **No long walks programmed** — walk if you want, but it's not required.
- **No barbell anything** — the trip is the no-lifting window by design.
- **No testing** — no max-depth mobility challenges, no long handstand holds, no "just one set" of anything that creates fatigue.

If you find yourself wanting to do "a little something more," the highest-leverage choice is **a longer walk** — true zone 1 cardio that doesn't compete with the rehab focus and helps recovery. Anything beyond that and we're back into Tier 3 (which we agreed not to do).

## What to Expect on Return (Wed May 6)

After 6 days off lifting:
- **Strength:** essentially intact (it takes 2+ weeks to lose meaningful strength in trained athletes)
- **Neural skill:** slightly degraded — the first heavy session back will feel "rusty," not "weak"
- **CNS readiness:** fresh and high — you'll feel mentally ready
- **Right erector:** if you've done the protocol daily, the right erector tonus should be measurably better than Apr 29 pre-trip
- **Left hip ER:** should still be in the closing trend
- **Hydration:** travel often dehydrates — emphasize the 500 ml + carbs pre-session protocol on May 6 even more than usual

The May 6 session is intentionally deloaded ~10% to handle the rust factor cleanly. **Don't try to "make up" for the trip with extra effort.** Trust the schedule.

> [!info]- May 6 Readiness Checklist (click to expand)
>
> Before the first post-trip lifting session, look at the tracking table:
>
> - If right erector post-score is mostly 4–5 → management protocol worked; proceed with May 6 as written.
> - If right erector is mostly 3 but not worsening → proceed with May 6 as written, but keep the belt strict and avoid any extra work.
> - If right erector is 1–2 or worsening across the trip → May 6 front squat stays at 100 kg or lower and OHS becomes empty-bar/light technique only.
> - If left hip ER is 4–5 by May 5 → left hip stiffness is likely ready to come off active watch after Wk 5 if lifting confirms it.
> - If left overhead score is still 2–3 → keep banded overhead distraction in the warm-up May 6–8, not just accessory.

## Red-Flag Boundaries — When to Modify or Stop

- **Right erector pain getting WORSE during trip:** stop the foam roll dose, keep just the breathing + couch stretch + cat-cow. Note it.
- **New pain anywhere (knee, shoulder, anything):** stop the protocol movement that triggered it, log what happened, modify or skip on subsequent days.
- **Sharp / electrical / referring pain in the lumbar:** stop everything, log, see a clinician on return if it persists.
- **Anterior shoulder pinch during banded work:** reduce band tension and angle immediately. If it persists, skip dislocates and overhead distraction that day; keep face pulls only.
- **Left knee discomfort during couch stretch or pigeon:** pad the knee, reduce the angle, or switch to a standing hip-flexor stretch. The trip protocol should not irritate the patellar tracking issue.

If anything character-changes during the trip (sharp instead of dull, referring instead of local, persistent instead of moving through the day), the management plan changes — message me when you're back if anything like that happens.

## Why This Matters — One Paragraph

The trip is not "lost training time." It's **structured recovery time during which the two active maintenance items get more deliberate attention than they would during a normal training week.** The right erector tonus and the left hip ER closing trend will both be in better shape on May 6 than they are on Apr 29 *if* you do the daily protocol. This is the entire job. Twenty minutes a day.
