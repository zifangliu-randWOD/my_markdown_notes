# Trip Mobility & Rehab Protocol — Apr 30 → May 5, 2026

[[00 - Program Overview]] | [[Phase 1 - Weeks 5-8]]

> [!tip] Trip Focus
> **6 days off lifting. The trip is your built-in mid-Phase deload between two heavy 3-day blocks.** Equipment: bodyweight + light resistance band(s). Activity tier: Tier 2 — mobility & rehab, no conditioning. The two active maintenance items are (1) **right erector spinae tonus** (newly characterized Wk 4 Fri — chronic muscular pattern) and (2) **left hip ER stiffness** (closing trend). Both respond well to the daily protocol below. The thoracic + shoulder bilateral restriction (worse on left) also gets meaningful attention from the banded distractions and dislocates. **The protocol is ~20 min/day. Do it every day. That's the whole job.**

## Daily Protocol — Same Sequence, Every Day

> [!info] Time required: ~20–25 min total. Order matters — joint mobility before stretching, breathing throughout.

### Phase 1 — Reset (3 min)

- [ ] **90/90 breathing — 2 min**
  - Lying on back, feet on wall, knees and hips both at 90°
  - Breathe into the posterior ribcage, allow lumbar to flatten into the floor
  - 8–10 slow breath cycles
  - *Why:* the foundational pelvic reset that resolved the lumbar-pelvic constraint in Wk 3. Also the diaphragmatic activation that supports right erector relaxation.

- [ ] **Right lumbar erector self-massage — 60 sec**
  - No foam roller? Use a tennis ball, a water bottle, or just your thumb/knuckle pressed against the wall
  - Find the right erector (rope-like muscle 2–3 cm right of the spine, between bottom rib and iliac crest)
  - Apply firm but not aggressive pressure. Roll/work for 60 seconds.
  - *Why:* manages the chronic right erector tonus pattern characterized Wk 4 Fri.

### Phase 2 — Hip Mobility (5 min)

- [ ] **Couch stretch — 60 sec each side**
  - Standing or kneeling — back foot elevated on a chair/bed/wall, front foot forward in a lunge
  - Squeeze the back-leg glute, posteriorly tilt the pelvis, push hips forward
  - You should feel a deep stretch in the front of the back-leg hip flexor and quad
  - *Why:* anteriorly tight hip flexors anteriorly tilt the pelvis → erectors stay loaded all day. Couch stretch directly releases the pelvis. **Highest-leverage movement in the protocol.**

- [ ] **Pigeon stretch — 60 sec each side, LEFT FIRST**
  - Front shin roughly parallel to the front of the mat, back leg extended behind, hips square
  - Sink the front hip toward the floor; for deeper stretch, fold torso forward over the front leg
  - *Why:* left hip ER (external rotation) was the closing finding in Wk 4. Keep it closed during the trip.

- [ ] **90/90 hip rotation — 10 reps each side**
  - Sit on floor, both legs at 90° (front leg externally rotated, back leg internally rotated)
  - Rotate the pelvis to switch sides (the back knee tips forward)
  - Slow and controlled, 10 reps each side
  - *Why:* maintains hip rotational mobility (ER + IR) needed for snatch/clean receive positions.

### Phase 3 — Thoracic + Shoulder (4 min)

- [ ] **Banded overhead distraction — 30 sec each arm, +10 sec on left**
  - Anchor band overhead (door, tree branch, sturdy fixture)
  - Grip band with one hand, thumb up, step away until band is taut and pulling arm forward and up
  - Allow arm into end-range overhead flexion; keep ribcage down (don't arch lower back)
  - Right: 30 sec. **Left: 40 sec.** (Left is the more restricted side.)
  - *Why:* shoulder joint capsule + thoracic mobility — directly targets the bilateral-worse-on-left thoracic restriction.

- [ ] **Banded shoulder dislocates — 10 reps slow**
  - Stand holding band wide grip (or PVC if you have one); slowly pass band overhead and behind, then return
  - Shoulders stay packed, don't shrug
  - 10 reps with a 3-second pace each direction
  - *Why:* thoracic extension under controlled shoulder flexion — front rack mobility carryover.

- [ ] **Banded face pulls — 15 reps**
  - Anchor band at face height. Pull the band toward your face with elbows high, end position is hands by the ears with elbows spread wide.
  - Mid-trap + posterior shoulder activation
  - *Why:* prevents posterior chain detraining during the lifting break. Carries directly to jerk lockout stability when you return.

### Phase 4 — Spinal Decompression (3 min)

- [ ] **Cat-cow — 10 reps, slow**
  - On hands and knees, alternate spinal flexion (cow → arch back, look up) and extension (cat → round back, tuck chin)
  - Move one vertebra at a time, breathing with each transition
  - *Why:* segmental spinal mobility — opens the lumbar joints between heavy training blocks.

- [ ] **Standing forward fold — 60 sec**
  - Feet hip-width, slowly hinge forward, let the head and arms hang
  - Slight bend in the knees if the hamstrings are very tight
  - Just hang. Breathe. Let the spine decompress.

### Optional Add-Ons (if you have band tension and a few extra minutes)

- [ ] **Banded supine hip distraction — 60 sec each side**
  - Lie on back, band looped around one foot, band anchored to a low fixed point
  - Let the band pull the hip forward (toward distraction), allowing capsule space
  - Especially helpful if left hip ER feels rough on a given day

- [ ] **Banded ankle dorsiflexion — 60 sec each side**
  - Band around the front of the ankle, anchored low and forward
  - In a lunge, drive the knee over the toes; band pulls the tibia forward
  - Carries forward to squat depth on return

## What's Deliberately NOT in This Protocol

- **No conditioning** — no bodyweight squats for sets, no push-ups for sets, no planks for time. You explicitly chose Tier 2.
- **No bodyweight strength work** — no goblet squats, no push-up variations as exercises.
- **No long walks programmed** — walk if you want, but it's not required.
- **No barbell anything** — the trip is the no-lifting window by design.

If you find yourself wanting to do "a little something more," the highest-leverage choice is **a longer walk** — true zone 1 cardio that doesn't compete with the rehab focus and helps recovery. Anything beyond that and we're back into Tier 3 (which we agreed not to do).

---

## What to Expect on Return (Wed May 6)

After 6 days off lifting:
- **Strength:** essentially intact (it takes 2+ weeks to lose meaningful strength in trained athletes)
- **Neural skill:** slightly degraded — the first heavy session back will feel "rusty," not "weak"
- **CNS readiness:** fresh and high — you'll feel mentally ready
- **Right erector:** if you've done the protocol daily, the right erector tonus should be measurably better than Apr 29 pre-trip
- **Left hip ER:** should still be in the closing trend
- **Hydration:** travel often dehydrates — emphasize the 500 ml + carbs pre-session protocol on May 6 even more than usual

The May 6 session is intentionally deloaded ~10% to handle the rust factor cleanly. **Don't try to "make up" for the trip with extra effort.** Trust the schedule.

---

## Red-Flag Boundaries — When to Modify or Stop

- **Right erector pain getting WORSE during trip:** stop the foam roll dose, keep just the breathing + couch stretch + cat-cow. Note it.
- **New pain anywhere (knee, shoulder, anything):** stop the protocol movement that triggered it, log what happened, modify or skip on subsequent days.
- **Sharp / electrical / referring pain in the lumbar:** stop everything, log, see a clinician on return if it persists.

If anything character-changes during the trip (sharp instead of dull, referring instead of local, persistent instead of moving through the day), the management plan changes — message me when you're back if anything like that happens.

---

## Why This Matters — One Paragraph

The trip is not "lost training time." It's **structured recovery time during which the two active maintenance items get more deliberate attention than they would during a normal training week.** The right erector tonus and the left hip ER closing trend will both be in better shape on May 6 than they are on Apr 29 *if* you do the daily protocol. This is the entire job. Twenty minutes a day.
