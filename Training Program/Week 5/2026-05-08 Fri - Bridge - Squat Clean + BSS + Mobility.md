# Friday — Week 5: Bridge Session — Squat Clean + BSS + Mobility

[[00 - Program Overview]] | [[Phase 1 - Weeks 5-8]]

> [!tip] Day Focus
> **This is a bridge session — not a stimulus session and not a recovery session.** It's the last positional dose before Monday's Wk 6 push window opens, and the work has three jobs, all structural rather than developmental: 12 paused squat cleans at 95 kg, a BSS dose at 17 kg if Wednesday's test held, and the full lumbar/hip mobility circuit. The squat clean piece extends the catch-up arc that's been a Phase 1 theme — Wk 3 Sat 20 reps → Wk 4 Wed 6 → Wk 5 Wed 5 → today 12 = **43 squat clean reps across four bridge sessions**, every one of them existing because Mon–Thu of most Phase 1 weeks ran ~0 squat cleans through the box-power-clean Wednesdays; today extends the Wk 3 Sat model exactly — light load, paused receive, every rep a positional check, not a strength rep. The BSS sits on a real progression decision: Wk 3 Sat at 15 kg was RPE 7→7.5, Wk 5 Wed retested 17 kg, and today is the held-or-not call — 17 kg locked in for the rest of Phase 1 if Wednesday came in at RPE ≤7, back to 15 kg for all three sets if it didn't. At 95 kg there is no stimulus — 68% of the clean PR is light, and the 1-sec pause at receive is positional, not loaded; **the point is habit-building**. The heavy clean stand-up breathing protocol applies even at 95 kg because the only way it becomes automatic at 110+ kg is to drill it cold, and the bar-drop-from-front-rack habit applies for the same reason — build the reflex at the load where it's optional so it's there at the load where it isn't. RPE governance is strict: anything above 6 on a clean today is the signal to stop and assess, not push through — this is not a session to chase numbers in. After the bar work, give the mobility circuit real time and use the comparison opportunities — thoracic depth on the bench-extension, left arm in the banded overhead distraction, left hip in pigeon — against the Wk 4 Thursday baselines; a full week of cumulative mobility plus the trip protocol should show as noticeably more range, not just "the same" range. Wk 6 opens Monday May 11 on snatch complex 6×1 @ 82 kg + BS 5×5 @ 146 kg, on a clean foundation with no new findings to monitor — the anterior shoulder closed, the right erector tonus characterized and managed, the BSS progression decided — and the +2–3% intensity bump fully earned by everything Wk 5 just delivered.

> [!info]- Squat Clean Catch-Up — Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Why this session:**
> The Phase 1 squat clean structural gap (power-clean-dominant Wednesdays for Wk 1–4) has been partially addressed by Wk 3 Sat (20 reps), Wk 4 Wed (6 reps), Wk 5 Wed (5 reps). Today adds 12 more reps in the receiving position.
>
> **Why 95 kg:**
> 68% of PR = light enough to focus entirely on receiving position quality, heavy enough to be a real squat clean (not a positional drill). With the 1-sec pause at the bottom, every rep is a positional check.
>
> **The 1-sec pause at the bottom of the squat catch:**
> - Receive in full squat
> - Pause 1 second — feel the position: elbows up, chest tall, back angle vertical, hips below parallel
> - Stand fully (with breathing protocol applied — controlled exhale)
> - Drop the bar to the platform from front rack
>
> **Bar drop protocol — habit-building:**
> Even at 95 kg, drop the bar from the front rack between reps. The Wk 4 review's hypothesis is that catch-at-pelvis on the descent stresses the anterior shoulder; building the drop-from-rack habit at moderate loads makes it automatic at heavy loads.
>
> **RPE target:** 5–6. If a set hits RPE 7, that's the signal to stop and assess. This is a maintenance-quality session, not a stimulus session.
