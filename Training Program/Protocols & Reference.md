## Deload Week Protocol (General Template)

> [!info] When: Weeks 4, 8, 12, 16, 20
> Purpose: Dissipate fatigue, allow tendons and CNS to recover, maintain movement patterns.

**Principles:**
- Oly lifts: 70-75%, focus on speed and positions, volume cut by 40-50%
- Squats: 60-70% for easy triples, no grinders
- Accessory/prehab: maintained at normal volume (tendons like consistency)
- Metcons: optional, keep it fun and light
- Mobility: increased — use the freed-up training time
- RPE should never exceed 6 on any day

**Signs you need an unscheduled deload:**
- Persistent joint pain that doesn't resolve with warm-up
- Missing lifts at 85% that you normally make
- Sleep quality declining despite no lifestyle changes
- Motivation consistently low for 3+ days
- Lingering muscle soreness beyond 72 hours

If any of these appear mid-block, insert a deload week and shift the schedule forward.

## Test Week Protocol (General Template)

> [!info] When: Weeks 8, 16, 24
> Purpose: Benchmark progress, establish new percentage bases.

**3 Days Before Testing:**
- Reduce volume to minimum (3x2 @ 70% on Oly lifts, 3x3 @ 65% on squats)
- Increase sleep to 8+ hours
- Increase carbohydrate intake slightly
- No new foods, no alcohol
- Hydrate well

**Test Day Rules:**
1. Full warm-up: 15 min minimum
2. Planned jumps only — write them down before you start
3. Jump sizes: ~10% jumps early, ~3-5% jumps above 80%
4. 1 rep at each weight. Move up or stop.
5. Rest 2-3 min between attempts above 80%
6. If you miss: retry once. If you miss again, that's your max for today.
7. Film every attempt above 85%

**Attempt Selection Guide:**
- Opener (~85%): a weight you never miss, even on a bad day
- 2nd attempt (~92-95%): a weight you've hit in training recently
- 3rd attempt (~98-102%): your target PR

**After Testing:**
- Record all results in the tracking sections
- Update percentage bases for the next phase
- Note what felt good and what felt off — this informs the next phase

## Mobility & Prehab Exercise Library

### Thoracic Mobility

**Thoracic Extension Over Foam Roller**
- Lie face up with foam roller across mid-back
- Hands behind head, elbows together
- Extend over the roller, hold 2-3 sec, return
- 3 x 10 breaths (extend on exhale)
- Move roller up/down to hit different segments

**Thoracic Extension Over Bench with Barbell**
- Kneel facing a bench, elbows on bench holding empty bar or PVC
- Sink chest toward floor, pushing hips back
- Hold end range for 2-3 breaths, return
- 3 x 10 breaths

**Thoracic Rotation on Wall (Open Book)**
- Side-lying, knees stacked at 90°
- Top arm reaches overhead and rotates to opposite side
- Follow hand with eyes, keep knees stacked
- 2 x 8 each side

**Prone Y-T-W Raises**
- Lie face down on bench or floor
- Y: arms overhead at 45°, thumbs up, lift and hold 2 sec
- T: arms straight out to sides, lift and hold
- W: elbows bent 90°, squeeze shoulder blades, lift and hold
- 3 x 8-10 each position

### Front Rack Mobility

**Banded Front Rack Stretch**
- Attach band to rig at elbow height
- Loop around wrist, step back to create tension
- Get into front rack position, let band pull wrist into extension
- 2 x 45-60 sec each side

**Banded Lat Stretch**
- Attach band overhead, grip with one hand
- Step away, hinge at hip, let band pull arm overhead
- Rotate torso slightly away from band for deeper stretch
- 2 x 45 sec each side

**Barbell Front Rack Hold**
- Load bar to ~40% of clean
- Hold in front rack position for 30-60 sec
- Focus: elbows as high as possible, full grip relaxed
- 3 x 30-60 sec

### Knee Rehab / Left Side

**Peterson Step-Downs**
- Stand on edge of box (15-20 cm) on working leg
- Slowly lower opposite heel to floor by bending working knee
- Control the descent — 3 sec down, tap heel, drive back up
- Key: knee tracks over toes, no collapse inward
- 3 x 10-12 each leg (left side first, match reps on right)

**Spanish Squat Isometric Hold**
- Loop heavy band around rig at knee height
- Step into band so it sits behind both knees
- Lean back into band, sink into quarter-squat position
- Hold — this loads the patellar tendon with reduced joint stress
- 3 x 30-45 sec

**Bulgarian Split Squat**
- Rear foot elevated on bench
- Front foot far enough forward that knee stays over ankle
- Controlled descent, drive through front heel
- 3 x 8-12 each leg (left first)
- Progress: bodyweight → DB → barbell

**Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift**
- Stand on one leg, hinge at hip, reach DB toward floor
- Keep back flat, slight bend in standing knee
- Feel hamstring stretch, drive hips forward to return
- 3 x 8-10 each leg
- Key for left side: focus on balance and control

### Lumbar-Pelvic / Core Reset

**90/90 Breathing (Daily — Added Week 2)**
- Lie on back, feet on a wall, knees and hips at 90 degrees
- Exhale fully through mouth, flattening lower back into the floor
- Hold flat-back position for 3-4 breath cycles (inhale nose, exhale mouth)
- 2 minutes total (3-4 sets of breath cycles)
- Purpose: teaches deep core to posteriorly tilt the pelvis against chronic erector/stabilizer stiffness
- Context: addresses the lumbar-pelvic constraint identified at squat depth under load (anterior pelvic tilt pattern). Also helps with the chronic lower lumbar stiffness that causes discomfort when lying flat.
- Do first in every warm-up — resets pelvic position before loading

**Goblet Squat Hold with Exhale**
- Hold bottom of a goblet squat (light KB/DB)
- Exhale fully, let the lower back "open" and flatten
- Hold for 3-5 breaths
- Optional add-on after 90/90 breathing if time allows
- Rehearses the lumbar-pelvic transition at depth under light load with conscious control

### Hip Mobility

**Pigeon Stretch**
- Front shin roughly parallel to hips (adjust angle for comfort)
- Sink hips toward floor, walk hands forward
- Hold and breathe — don't force depth
- 2 x 45-60 sec each side

**90/90 Hip Rotation**
- Sit with both legs at 90° angles
- Front leg: shin parallel in front of you
- Back leg: shin parallel behind you
- Rotate from one side to the other, keeping torso tall
- 2 x 8-10 each direction

**Banded Hip Flexor Stretch**
- Half-kneeling, band around back hip pulling forward
- Squeeze glute on kneeling side, lean slightly forward
- 2 x 45-60 sec each side

### Shoulder Prehab

**External Rotation with Band**
- Band attached at elbow height
- Elbow pinned to side at 90°
- Rotate forearm outward against band resistance
- 3 x 12-15 each arm

**Banded Pull-Aparts**
- Hold band at shoulder width, arms straight in front
- Pull apart to chest level, squeeze shoulder blades
- 3 x 15-20

**Banded Overhead Distraction**
- Attach band overhead, loop around wrist
- Step away, let band pull arm into overhead position
- Rotate and explore end range
- 2 x 30 sec each arm

### Ankle Mobility

**Wall Ankle Dorsiflexion**
- Face wall, foot ~10 cm away
- Drive knee forward over toes toward wall
- Keep heel on ground
- 2 x 30 sec each side
- Progress by moving foot further from wall

## Gymnastics Skill Progressions

### Ring Muscle-Up Progression

> [!info] Prerequisites: bar muscle-ups, strict ring dips, false grip hang

**Phase 1 — Build Components (Weeks 1-4)**
1. Deep ring dips: 3 x 5-8 (slow 3 sec negative)
2. False grip hang: 3 x 15-20 sec
3. Banded ring MU transitions: 3 x 3 (heavy band)

**Phase 2 — Strengthen Transition (Weeks 5-8)**
1. Deep ring dips: 3 x 8 (add light weight if easy)
2. False grip pull-ups: 3 x 3-5
3. Banded ring MU transitions: 4 x 2-3 (reduce band)
4. Attempt 1 unassisted transition (Week 6+)

**Phase 3 — First Rep (Weeks 9-12)**
1. Ring MU attempts: 5-10 per session (full rest between)
2. If you get it: practice singles with full rest
3. False grip pull to sternum: 3 x 3 (if still working on it)

**Phase 4 — Build Volume (Weeks 13-24)**
1. Once you have 1 rep: build to sets of 2-3
2. Practice kipping rhythm for efficiency
3. Goal: 3 x 3 unbroken by end of program

**Common Fixes:**
- Can't hold false grip → more false grip hangs, use chalk
- Get stuck in the transition → focus on pulling to sternum, not chin
- Elbows flare in the dip → more deep ring dip work, slow negatives

### Handstand Walk Progression

> [!info] Prerequisites: strict HSPU, 30+ sec wall handstand hold

**Phase 1 — Build Balance (Weeks 1-4)**
1. Wall-facing handstand hold: 3 x 30-40 sec
2. Wall walk with shoulder taps: 3 x 5-8 each hand
3. Freestanding handstand kick-up attempts: 5 per session

**Phase 2 — Weight Shifting (Weeks 5-8)**
1. Wall-facing HS hold: 3 x 40-45 sec
2. Wall-facing HS lateral weight shifts: 3 x 5-6 each direction
3. Freestanding HS hold attempts: 5 x max hold (aim for 5-8 sec)
4. First steps off wall: 5 attempts

**Phase 3 — First Steps (Weeks 9-12)**
1. HS walk off wall: 5 attempts (aim for 3-5 steps)
2. If walking: practice 3-5m distances
3. Freestanding HS hold: 3 x max hold

**Phase 4 — Build Distance (Weeks 13-24)**
1. HS walk: 5 x max distance
2. Practice turns and direction changes
3. Practice under light fatigue (after HSPU)
4. Goal: consistent 10m+ walks by end of program

**Common Fixes:**
- Falling over (past balance point) → more wall holds, focus on finger pressure
- Can't find balance → practice kick-ups to freestanding, not against wall
- Shoulders fatigue fast → more strict HSPU volume, overhead holds with weight
