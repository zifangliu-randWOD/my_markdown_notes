## Key Wins

1. **84 kg snatch single @ RPE 7** — new Phase 1 program high. Bar driven high, hook grip, smooth squat-receive catch. Achieved on a flat + hungry day at the end of peak volume week — the strength ceiling is clearly not the limiter.
2. **Lumbar-pelvic constraint resolved** — absent on Thursday's 107 kg front squat (5×5, 3-0-0 tempo). Had been a limiter since Week 1. Two weeks of consistent 90/90 breathing + accumulated squat exposure removed it.
3. **Breathing strategy validated on both squats** — back squat (110 kg 5×5) and front squat (107 kg 5×5) both benefiting from between-rep breathing. RPE dropped ~1–1.5 points vs Week 2. Mechanism confirmed: the elevated RPE in Weeks 1–2 was a breathing/bracing error, not a load calibration issue.
4. **Jerk dip improvement** — wider stance (+5 cm outside hip-width) naturally shifts COM toward the heels, reducing forward knee loading and knee pain. Found Tuesday, confirmed helpful.
5. **BSS Week 3 minimum completed** — 3×10 each leg at 15 kg with 3s descending tempo, RPE 7–7.5. First full BSS session completed in the regular training week structure (via Saturday makeup).
6. **Anterior shoulder finding investigated** — not reproduced at 85 kg on Saturday. Likely load/fatigue dependent rather than structural. No acute concern.

- Diagnosis: snatch ceiling is technique-limited, not strength-limited. The smooth 84 kg single on a flat day confirms the strength is there. Bar-forward failures under fatigue are a second-pull patience issue, not a ceiling.

## Recurring Technical Patterns

### Pattern 1 — Eye Focus / Gaze Protocol (ACTIVE — being addressed)

Raised in 4 of 6 sessions (Mon BS, Wed clean, Thu FS + OHS, Sat squat clean). Protocol now established:

| Movement | Torso angle | Gaze target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Back squat | Forward lean | Floor 2–3m ahead; gaze follows torso angle naturally |
| Front squat / OHS / Clean catch | Upright | Wall at eye level, 2–3m ahead |
| Snatch / clean pull | Forward lean → vertical | Eyes forward, chest aimed high; do NOT look down under load |

Action: build this in warm-up sets before loading. Saturday's deliberate squat clean practice is the model — "eyes on the wall" as a setup habit before every pull.

### Pattern 2 — COM Forward in Jerk Dip (ACTIVE — fix confirmed)

Present since Week 2. Wider stance (+5 cm) naturally reduces it. Wider jerk grip also reduces hand-repositioning friction. Both confirmed positive on Tuesday's jerk sets 5–6.
**Default jerk stance from Week 5: +5 cm wider than hip-width on all jerk work.**

### Pattern 3 — Chest Drop Under Heavy Clean/Snatch (ACTIVE)

First flagged Wednesday at the box (HPC to 120 kg: eyes look down, chest drops, back rounds on the catch). Confirmed Friday on snatch working sets (bar-forward failures at 80 kg).
**Cue: "chest to the ceiling" on second pull extension. Practice from 70 kg warm-up sets — not just the heavy work.**

### Pattern 4 — Squat Clean Pattern Decay (ACTIVE — partially corrected)

Three consecutive Wednesdays of power-clean-dominant box sessions have degraded the squat clean receiving pattern. Friday set 4 ("almost forgot how to catch the bar") is the diagnostic moment. Saturday's 15 positional reps at 85 kg partially restored it.
**Structural fix needed for Weeks 5–8 — see programming notes.**

### Pattern 5 — Bar Forward on Fatigued Snatch Working Sets (NEW)

Three misses on Friday's 80 kg snatch: all via "bar fell forward" in the catch. Fatigue signature — the second pull loses its vertical component under accumulated load, yanking the bar forward.
**Fix: stay patient in the pull, finish the hip drive before pulling under. If a rep is missed, note it and move on — no compensation sets at 75%+ when fatigued.**

## New Findings Requiring Follow-Up

### Finding 1 — Anterior Shoulder Pull on Heavy Squat Clean

| | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| When | Friday, 98 kg C&J squat clean pull |
| What | Stretch/pull sensation on anterior shoulder joint during pull-under |
| Comparison | NOT present on power cleans at 120 kg same week |
| Saturday test | NOT reproduced at 85 kg |
| Hypothesis | Forward bar path at heavy loads + decayed pull-under timing → anterior capsule stress on the "reach forward" catch |
| Week 5 action | Retest at 90–95 kg fresh. If reproduced: add band ER × 15 + sleeper stretch 60 sec to pre-clean warm-up |

| | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| When | Saturday warm-up, hip circles |
| What | "Rust gear" roughness on left hip ER only (left IR fine, right clear) |
