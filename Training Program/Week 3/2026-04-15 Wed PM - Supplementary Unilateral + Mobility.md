# Wednesday PM — Week 3: Supplementary Session

[[00 - Program Overview]] | [[Phase 1 - Weeks 1-4]]

> [!tip] Day Focus
> This is a **supplementary session** to cover the unilateral and mobility work missed from this morning's box session. Three items: (1) **Bulgarian split squats** — the most critical item, now at 15 kg KBs (up from 12 kg in Week 2 PM), (2) **single-leg RDLs** — balance and posterior chain, and (3) **banded hip flexor stretch** — now at 60 sec per side (up from 45 sec). This session should take **20-25 minutes** and feel like moderate accessory work. Your posterior chain already worked this morning (tempo DLs and pause DLs), so the quads and hip flexors are the focus here.

## Bulgarian Split Squat — MANDATORY

3 x 10 each leg (15 kg KB each hand)
*Week 2 PM session used 12 kg and both legs were RPE 6. This week: try 15 kg. If the left leg can't handle 15 kg at RPE 7 or below, drop to 12 kg for left only.*

| Set | Leg   | Weight | Reps  | RPE | Notes |
| --- | ----- | ------ | ----- | --- | ----- |
| 1   | Left  | 15 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 1   | Right | 15 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 2   | Left  | 15 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 2   | Right | 15 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 3   | Left  | 15 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 3   | Right | 15 kg  | /10   |     |       |

> [!info]- Bulgarian Split Squat — Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Why 15 kg?**
> Week 2 PM session: both legs at RPE 6 with 12 kg KBs. The load was clearly sub-threshold — you need progressive overload for the VMO/quad to adapt. 15 kg is a 25% increase in external load, which should push RPE to 6.5-7.5 on the left leg. If left leg hits RPE 8+, drop to 12 kg for the left side — the right side stays at 15 kg.
>
> **Execution:**
> - Rear foot on bench, stand ~2 feet away
> - Hold a KB in each hand at your sides
> - **Left leg first** — always train the weaker side first
> - Descend until back knee nearly touches the floor
> - Front knee tracks over toes — 15-20 degree toe-out to protect the patellar tracking
> - Drive back up through front heel
> - Rest 60-90 sec between legs, 90 sec between sets
>
> **Left knee management:**
> - If patellar pain appears, check foot position (more toe-out)
> - Stop 2 inches higher if pain at full depth
> - RPE target: 6.5-7.5 per leg. Note any left-right gap.
>
> **After this morning's session:**
> Your quads are relatively fresh — the morning session was mostly posterior chain (deadlifts) and upper-body power (cleans caught high). BSS should feel manageable.

## Single-Leg RDL

3 x 10 each leg (12 kg KB, contralateral)
*Week 2 PM: balance was shaky, hamstring tightness limited rear leg extension. Stay at 12 kg — focus on range of motion improvement, not load increase.*

| Set | Leg   | Weight | Reps  | RPE | Notes |
| --- | ----- | ------ | ----- | --- | ----- |
| 1   | Left  | 12 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 1   | Right | 12 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 2   | Left  | 12 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 2   | Right | 12 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 3   | Left  | 12 kg  | /10   |     |       |
| 3   | Right | 12 kg  | /10   |     |       |

> [!info]- Single-Leg RDL — Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Execution:**
> - Stand on one leg, hold KB in the opposite hand (contralateral loading)
> - Hinge forward at the hip, back flat, reaching the KB toward the floor
> - Free leg extends behind you — aim for a straight line from head to heel
> - Deep hamstring stretch on the standing leg, then drive hips forward to stand
> - Left leg first, match reps on right
>
> **After this morning's deadlifts:**
> Your hamstrings and lower back worked hard with tempo and paused pulls at 150-155 kg. SL-RDLs at 12 kg are a different stimulus — balance-dominant, light load, high rep. If your lower back feels tight, go lighter (8 kg or bodyweight) and focus on the hip hinge pattern. Freestanding — no wall support. The balance challenge IS the training stimulus.
>
> **Week 2 findings to build on:**
> Balance was shaky and hamstring tightness limited rear leg extension. Both improve with consistency. This is session 2 — expect slight improvement in balance but don't force depth.

## Banded Hip Flexor Stretch

2 x 60 sec each side
*Up from 45 sec in Week 2. Supports squat depth and upright torso position — directly addresses the hamstring/hip flexor tightness and lumbar-pelvic constraint.*

- [ ] Left side: 2 x 60 sec
- [ ] Right side: 2 x 60 sec

> [!info]- Hip Flexor Stretch — Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Execution:**
> - Attach a heavy band to a low rig point behind you
> - Step into the band so it sits in your hip crease
> - Step forward into a half-kneeling position
> - The band pulls your hip forward and down, creating traction
> - Squeeze the glute on the kneeling side, lean slightly forward
> - Hold and breathe deeply — 60 seconds per side, 2 rounds
>
> **Why 60 sec instead of 45?**
> This addresses the hamstring/hip flexor tightness connected to the lumbar-pelvic constraint found during Week 2 front squats and the SL-RDL range limitation. The extra 15 seconds per side accumulates over weeks.
