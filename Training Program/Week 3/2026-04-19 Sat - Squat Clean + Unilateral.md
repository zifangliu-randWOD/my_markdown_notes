## Primary: Squat Clean (Positional)

5 x 3 @ 60% (85 kg) — 2-sec pause in the catch
- **Squat-receive every rep.** Ride the bar to full front squat depth regardless of pull height. Even if you can power clean 85 kg easily, descend immediately to the bottom.
- **2-sec pause in the catch:** At the bottom of the front squat, hold for 2 seconds. Elbows high, torso vertical, breathe. Then stand.
- **Eye focus:** Pick a spot on the wall at eye level before the first pull. Keep your eyes there through the pull, turnover, and catch. This is the same "chest up, eyes forward" cue from Wednesday's heavy clean finding — now practiced deliberately at light load.
- Rest 1:30-2:00 between sets.
- If RPE exceeds 7: this is too heavy for positional work — drop to 80 kg.

> [!info]- Squat Clean (Positional) — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Why squat cleans on Saturday?**
> The squat clean receiving position is one of the most important skills in Phase 1. The program prescribes squat cleans every Wednesday and Friday. But all three Wednesdays have been power cleans at the box, and Wednesday's power-clean-to-max session explicitly showed the problem: at 130 kg you couldn't keep it as a power clean — the catch defaulted to a squat clean with a rounded back and dropped chest. That's what happens when the squat receiving position isn't trained regularly — under heavy load, the body defaults to it unprepared.
>
> **Why 85 kg (60%) with a pause?**
> Three reasons:
> 1. **Post-peak-volume fatigue.** This is day 6 of the hardest week in Phase 1. Heavy loading would be counterproductive — the body needs to recover before Monday's deload. 85 kg is light enough that pulling mechanics are easy and the focus stays on the catch.
> 2. **The pause forces receiving position ownership.** At 85 kg, you can power clean it and drop into a squat. The 2-sec pause at the bottom prevents rushing — you must sit in the front squat catch with elbows high, torso vertical, and eyes forward. This is the exact position you need at 130+ kg but can't practice there yet.
> 3. **Cue integration.** All week you've been collecting technique cues: "chest up, eyes on the wall" (Wed), "elbows UP" (Thu), eye focus protocols (Mon/Thu). At 85 kg, cognitive load is low enough to apply multiple cues simultaneously. At 105+ kg, you can only focus on one thing. Use the light weight to wire the habits.
>
> **The effective practice math:**
> 5 sets x 3 reps = 15 squat clean reps with deliberate catches. Combined with Friday's 5 squat cleans at 98 kg, that's 20 squat clean reps for the week. Not ideal (the program designed for 12 squat cleans on Wednesday + 5 on Friday = 17), but it corrects the zero-to-something deficit.
>
> **What to focus on, rep by rep:**
> 1. **Before the pull:** Eyes on the wall. Set the intent: "I am catching this in a full squat."
> 2. **Through the pull:** "Sweep the bar into the hip." Keep eyes on the wall — don't look down.
> 3. **At the turnover:** "Fast elbows." Whip the elbows through and UP. The bar should land on the deltoid shelf with elbows high.
> 4. **In the catch:** Ride to full depth. As soon as you hit the bottom, count "one-one-thousand, two-one-thousand." During the pause: check elbows (high?), check eyes (on the wall?), check torso (vertical?). Then stand.
> 5. **Between reps:** Reset fully. Touch and go is fine for metcons, not for positional work. Dead stop, re-grip, re-set eyes, pull again.
>
> **After Friday's heavy session:**
> Your body has been pulling and catching all week. At 85 kg, the nervous system demand is low. If anything feels off — lower back tight, grip fatigued, front rack stiff — that's end-of-week fatigue, not a problem with today's weight. The warm-up will tell you how the body feels. If the empty bar front squat feels heavy or stiff, take an extra set at 60 kg before jumping to 85 kg.

> The squat cleans before this will have pre-fatigued the quads slightly, but 85 kg for triples is not demanding enough to meaningfully affect BSS performance at 15 kg KBs. The quads should be warm and ready.

> **Execution:**
> - Rear foot on bench, stand ~2 feet away
> - Hold a KB in each hand at your sides
> - **Left leg first** — always train the weaker side first

> - Front knee tracks over toes — 15-20 degree toe-out
> - Drive back up through front heel
> - Rest 60-90 sec between legs, 90 sec between sets
>
> **Left knee management:**
> If patellar pain appears, check foot position first (more toe-out). Stop 2 inches higher if pain at full depth. If RPE 8+ on set 1, drop to 12 kg for the left side — right side stays at 15 kg.
