# Friday — Week 3: Snatch + C&J + HS Walk Skill

[[00 - Program Overview]] | [[Phase 1 - Weeks 1-4]]

> [!tip] Day Focus
> Friday is the **full lifts day** and the peak of the week. The snatch heavy single climbs to **84 kg (80%)** — a new program high and only 2 kg below your Week 1 Friday single. Working sets stay at 79 kg but this is after a heavier single than any previous week. The C&J goes to **98 kg** (75%, up from 95 kg) and adds a 5th set. This is the heaviest and most voluminous Friday of Phase 1 before the Week 4 deload. **Jerk grip experiment continues:** try cleaning with the wider grip on 1-2 sets to test whether that solves the hand-repositioning issue from Week 2. Handstand walk progressions add a new drill: **lateral weight shifts**. All the Week 2 cues carry forward: "sweep into hip," "knuckles to ceiling," "heels heavy through the dip."

## Warm-up (10 min)

- [ ] **90/90 breathing: 2 min** (feet on wall, flatten lower back into floor, 3-4 breath cycles per set)
- [ ] Snatch grip RDL x 5
- [ ] Hang snatch high pull x 5
- [ ] Snatch balance x 5
- [ ] Clean & jerk with empty bar x 3

> [!info]- Warm-up Movement Guide (click to expand)
>
> **90/90 Breathing — 2 min**
> Standard. Resets pelvic position before loading.
>
> **Snatch Grip RDL x5**
> Builds awareness of the pulling position. Feel hamstrings load as you hinge. Bar close to legs, shoulders over bar, back flat. Grooves the first pull position.
>
> **Hang Snatch High Pull x5**
> From the hip, extend hard, elbows up and out, bar to chest height. Primes explosive pulling. Think "fast elbows, high elbows."
>
> **Snatch Balance x5**
> Bar on back, dip and drive, punch under into full OHS. This is the final snatch warm-up drill and the most important for speed under the bar. Apply "knuckles to ceiling" on the catch.
>
> **Clean & Jerk with Empty Bar x3**
> Full movement rehearsal. On the jerk portion: apply "heels heavy through the dip" cue. If you're testing the wider jerk grip today, try it here too at empty bar.

> [!info]- Snatch (Full Lifts) — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Week 3 progression — heaviest snatch session of Phase 1:**
> - Heavy single: 79 kg (Wk1) → 81 kg (Wk2) → 84 kg (Wk3). That's 80% of your 105 kg max and only 1 kg below the snatch-to-bodyweight ratio threshold.
> - Working sets: consistent at 79 kg across all 3 weeks, but the context changes — after a heavier single, 79 kg should feel relatively lighter on the working sets.
>
> **Building to 84 kg:**
> Build up gradually with more intermediate steps than Week 2. Suggested: empty bar → 40 → 50 → 60 → 70 → 76 → 80 → 84 kg. The 80 kg single before 84 kg gives you a confidence check. If 80 kg doesn't feel right, stop there — 80 kg is still a Week 2+ heavy single.
>
> **84 kg considerations:**
> This is the heaviest snatch in the program so far. At 80%, there's less margin for positional error:
> - The bar MUST stay close (sweep into hip cue). Any forward drift at this load = missed lift.
> - The turnover must be fast. At 84 kg, you can't muscle it overhead — you must pull yourself under.
> - Squat-receive is now a necessity, not just a practice cue. 84 kg is likely too heavy to power snatch with good positions.
>
> **Working sets at 79 kg after 84 kg:**
> After a heavier single, working sets feel relatively lighter — this is a potentiation effect. RPE on the 79 kg doubles should be 6-7 (down from the 6.5-7 at 79 kg in Week 2). Use this to focus on execution quality: bar path, catch position, hook grip maintenance.
>
> **End-of-peak-volume fatigue:**
> This is day 5 of peak volume week. Monday's snatch complex (18 total reps), Tuesday's jerk + push press, Wednesday's clean + deadlift, Thursday's front squat + OHS + metcon — it's all accumulated. If you feel slower or less explosive than usual, that's normal. The Week 4 deload is designed to let this fatigue dissipate. Don't let a "heavy day" feeling deter you from attempting 84 kg — try it. If it doesn't work, take 80 kg as your single and move on.
>
> **Cue checklist per rep:**
> 1. Before the pull: "sweep into hip" — set the intent to keep the bar close
> 2. At the catch: "knuckles to ceiling" — press up with heel of hand
> 3. At the bottom: don't rush to stand. Stabilize, feel the position, then stand.

## Secondary: Clean & Jerk

5 x (1+1) @ 75% (98 kg working weight)
- Clean 75% = 105 kg / Jerk 75% = ~90 kg — compromise at 98 kg
- Focus: smooth transition from clean to jerk, reset feet between
- **Jerk grip experiment:** On sets 3-4, try **cleaning with the wider grip** (Option A from Week 2 review). This avoids the hand-repositioning problem. On sets 1-2 and 5, use standard clean grip.
- **Dip cue: "heels heavy through the dip"**

| 2   | 98 kg  |       | Clean |       |
| 3   | 98 kg  |       | Wide  |       |
| 4   | 98 kg  |       | Wide  |       |
| 5   | 98 kg  |       | Clean |       |

> [!info]- Clean & Jerk — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Week 3 progression:**
> From 95 kg to 98 kg (+3 kg) and from 4 sets to 5. At 98 kg:
> - Clean portion: 70% of 140 kg clean — should feel comfortable. RPE 5-6 on the clean.
> - Jerk portion: 82% of ~120 kg jerk — this is the harder component. RPE 7-8 on the jerk.
> - The clean-jerk RPE gap is expected and reflects the priority order: jerk mechanics are the limiter.
>
> **The wider grip experiment (sets 3-4):**
> Week 2 identified that repositioning hands from clean catch to wider jerk grip is difficult under load. Option A: clean with the wider grip to avoid the transition altogether.
>
> What to expect with a wider-grip clean:
> - The front rack will feel slightly different — hands wider means elbows may not get as high. At 98 kg this should still be manageable.
> - The pull itself is unchanged (you don't pull with your arms in a clean).
> - If the wider-grip clean catch feels unstable or the bar slides, go back to clean grip on set 4.
> - The payoff: if you can clean and jerk with the same wider grip, the transition is eliminated entirely.
>
> **Jerk from squat clean vs from rack:**
> Week 2 data: zero knee pain on the jerk after a squat clean (Friday, 95 kg), 2-3/10 pain from the rack (Thursday, 86 kg). Today all jerks follow a squat clean, which should set up better thoracic engagement. Track whether the pattern repeats.
>
> **Dip mechanics:**
> Same cues as Tuesday: "heels heavy through the dip," consistent depth. If Tuesday's dip calibration drill helped, use it during warm-up again. The 98 kg load is heavier than Tuesday's 90 kg jerk from the rack, but it's coming after a squat clean (which sets up the position better).
>
> **Expected RPE:**
> Clean: RPE 5-6. Jerk: RPE 7-8. If the jerk feels like RPE 9+, the peak volume week is catching up — drop to 93 kg for remaining sets.

## Gymnastics Skill: Handstand Walk Progressions (5-10 min)

- [ ] Wall-facing handstand hold: 3 x 40 sec
- [ ] Wall walk with shoulder taps: 3 x 8 each hand
- [ ] Freestanding handstand kick-up attempts: 5 attempts (try to hold 3-5 sec)
- [ ] Wall-facing HS lateral weight shifts: 3 x 5 each direction (new)

> [!info]- Handstand Walk Progressions — Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Week 3 progression:**
> - Wall holds: 35 → 40 sec. Shoulder endurance in the inverted position continues building. If 40 sec feels easy, move hands closer to the wall for a more vertical body.
> - Shoulder taps: 6 → 8 each hand. Two more reps per side. Go slow — control over speed.
> - Freestanding kick-ups: same 5 attempts. You hit a 5-second hold in Week 2. Don't chase it — just keep attempting and the holds will naturally extend. If you get another 3-5 sec hold, great. If not, the attempt IS the training.
> - **Lateral weight shifts (NEW):** Face the wall in a handstand position (hands ~6 inches from wall). Shift your weight to one hand, lifting the other hand 1-2 inches off the floor, then shift to the other side. 5 shifts per direction. This trains the exact lateral weight transfer needed for handstand walking. Start very small — just unweight one hand slightly. You don't need to lift it high.
>
> **Peak volume note:**
> This comes at the end of a full 5-day week — the most complete week of training since Week 1. Your shoulders have been worked hard (Tuesday push press/jerk, Wednesday pulls, Thursday OHS). If shoulder fatigue limits hold times, that's expected and informative — log actual times vs target.
>
> **Handstand walk is knee-friendly** — a welcome mental break after the heavy lifts.

## Accessory Window (15-20 min)

- [ ] Peterson step-downs: 3 x 12 each leg
  - **Left leg:** 10-15 cm step, 8-10 kg KB, vertical torso target
  - **Right leg:** 20 cm step, 8-10 kg KB, full ROM
- [ ] Pigeon stretch: 2 x 60 sec each side (up from 45 sec)
- [ ] 90/90 hip rotation: 2 x 10 each side (up from 8)

> [!info]- Accessory — Mechanism & Coaching Notes (click to expand)
>
> **Peterson Step-Downs 3x12 each leg (asymmetric protocol)**
> Same protocol as Monday. If Monday's session went well (12 reps, no pain), repeat the same load and step height. If Monday showed you can handle more (e.g., 8 kg felt very easy), progress to 10 kg KB on the left side. Right side can progress independently.
>
> **End-of-week comparison:** How does the left knee feel on Peterson step-downs today vs Monday? Is the eccentric control improving? Can you hold the descending phase as long or longer than Week 2 Friday?
>
> **Pigeon Stretch 2x60 sec each side**
> Up from 45 sec in Week 2. Targets deep external rotators. Get into pigeon position, keep hips square, fold forward. The extra 15 seconds per side adds to the hip mobility accumulation. Don't force depth — breathe and let gravity work.
>
> **90/90 Hip Rotation 2x10 each side**
> Up from 2x8 in Week 2. Two more reps per side. This was "hard, can't do it smoothly without wiggling" in Week 2. The goal isn't smooth yet — it's consistent practice. Focus on keeping the torso tall and owning whatever range you have. The wiggling will decrease with weeks of exposure.
